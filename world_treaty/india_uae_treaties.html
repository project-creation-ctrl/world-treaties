Section 03 — CEPA: India's Most Successful FTA

The CEPA: 88 Days to History

The India–UAE CEPA was negotiated in just 88 days — the fastest comprehensive FTA India has ever concluded. Signed February 18, 2022, in force May 1, 2022. Three years in, it is India's single most successful trade agreement: non-oil trade hit $100B in FY25, a year ahead of the 5-year target.

Coverage
📋
97.4% UAE Tariff Lines Duty-Free
UAE eliminates duties on 97.4% of tariff lines covering 99% of Indian exports by value. India offers duty elimination on 80%+ of tariff lines. Near-universal free trade for India — no other partner offers this breadth.
Services
💼
11 Service Sectors, 100+ Sub-Sectors
The most comprehensive services chapter in any India FTA. UAE offers 111 service sub-sectors to India; India offers 100 to UAE. Covers IT, professional services, financial services, education, healthcare, telecom, and construction.
Digital
💻
First-Ever Digital Trade Chapter
India's first-ever digital trade chapter in any FTA — covering e-commerce, electronic payments, cross-border data flows, and digital identity. Pioneering for India's FTA ambitions; sets the template for future agreements.
Pharma
💊
90-Day Pharma Fast-Track
CEPA includes a dedicated pharma annex allowing Indian drugs approved by recognised authorities to access UAE market within 90 days. Boosts India's pharmaceutical exports — one of the biggest non-oil beneficiaries.
Result
📈
$100B Non-Oil Trade — Target Hit Early
Non-oil bilateral trade reached $100B in FY 2024–25 — a year ahead of the 5-year CEPA target. Total bilateral trade (including oil) was $100B+ in FY24. The fastest trade transformation India has achieved with any partner.
Gateway
🌐
UAE as Africa + Europe Gateway
UAE's position under GAFTA (Great Arab Free Trade Area) gives India CEPA-backed access to 14 Arab countries. UAE's connectivity to Africa and Europe means the CEPA's real value exceeds the bilateral — it is a regional gateway agreement.
CEPA — PIL Classification

"The India–UAE CEPA is a comprehensive preferential trade agreement under WTO GATT Article XXIV — legally the most binding and institutionally the most structured trade instrument India has signed since the 2010 ASEAN CECA. Unlike India's earlier BITs (which India terminated en masse), the CEPA contains binding WTO-compatible dispute resolution. It also establishes a Joint Committee, Trade in Goods Committee, Services Sub-Committee, and Rules of Origin Committee — creating a permanent treaty governance architecture rather than a one-time agreement."

Section 05 — Defence: The New Frontier

From Exercises to Strategic Defence Partnership

India–UAE defence ties have moved from basic training cooperation (early 2000s) to joint exercises, counter-terrorism coordination, and — as of January 2026 — a formal Strategic Defence Partnership Letter of Intent. The 13th JDCC in July 2025 was held at Secretary level for the first time, signalling a step-change in strategic weight.

Army
⚔️
Exercise Desert Cyclone
First bilateral army exercise between India and UAE — held Rajasthan, January 2024 (Desert Cyclone-I). Desert Cyclone-II held in UAE, December 2025. Marks army-level military interoperability as a new pillar of bilateral defence cooperation.
Air Force
✈️
Desert Knight & Desert Flag
Desert Knight: First trilateral India–UAE–France air exercise (UAE, Jan 2024). Desert Flag: India's largest IAF contingent (with 11 other air forces) participated in April–May 2025. IAF also participated in multilateral Tarang Shakti and MILAN exercises.
Navy
⚓
Gulf Waves Naval Exercise
Bilateral Naval Exercise Gulf Waves conducted October 2024. Indian Coast Guard–UAE National Guard MoU on maritime security signed July 2025 at 13th JDCC. Covers SAR, anti-piracy, pollution response, and real-time Indian Ocean intelligence sharing.
Landmark
🤝
Defence Partnership — Letter of Intent (2026)
Historic LoI signed during UAE President MbZ's January 2026 visit to New Delhi. Covers joint training, technology transfer, drone and surveillance technologies, and defence interoperability. Precursor to a formal bilateral defence treaty — India's first in the Gulf.
Counter-Terror
🛡️
Joint Counter-Terrorism Operations
December 2020: India and UAE jointly foiled a Pakistani ISI-backed Khalistani–Hizbul Mujahideen attack in Delhi. Cell head arrested in UAE and deported to India. Real-time intelligence sharing between India's agencies and UAE has been operationally consequential.
13th JDCC
🏛️
Joint Defence Cooperation Committee
13th JDCC held July 2025 in New Delhi — first ever at Secretary level (India's Defence Secretary + UAE Under Secretary Lt Gen Ibrahim Al Alawi). Expanded into maritime, customised training, and advanced defence technology co-development domains.
Geopolitical Context — Why Defence Now?

"India's defence partnership with UAE accelerates precisely as Pakistan's military footprint in the Gulf expands. Pakistan signed the Saudi–Pakistan Strategic Mutual Defence Agreement in September 2025; Islamabad is now involved in Gaza stabilisation forces and Yemeni conflict architecture. India's Defence LoI with UAE is a direct strategic counterweight — positioning India as the Gulf's preferred democratic defence partner against Pakistan's growing military-commercial presence."

Final PIL Conclusion — A New Model of Gulf Bilateralism

"India–UAE represents the most complete bilateral treaty architecture India has constructed with any Gulf state — and arguably with any developing-world partner. The CEPA provides hard-law trade obligations; the Comprehensive Strategic Partnership provides political-diplomatic governance; the JDCC and Defence LoI provide security scaffolding; the I2U2 and IMEC provide multilateral embeddedness; the UPI/RuPay integration provides financial architecture; and the IIT/IIM campuses provide knowledge-economy anchoring. This is not one treaty — it is a treaty ecosystem, designed to be self-reinforcing. In PIL terms, the India–UAE model has moved beyond state consent to treaty obligation toward something more durable: institutional lock-in."
