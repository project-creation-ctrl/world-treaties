Section 02 — Bilateral Framework

Every India-Pakistan treaty was born from war, crisis, or international pressure. Each has either survived against the odds or collapsed under the weight of geopolitical mistrust.

2025 Diplomatic Collapse

"For the first time in 65 years, the Indus Waters Treaty — which survived three wars, Kargil, and countless crises — has been suspended. Pakistan has placed the Simla Agreement in abeyance. Both countries have closed borders, expelled diplomats, and halted trade. 2025 marks the most complete breakdown of the India-Pakistan treaty architecture since partition."

Section 03 — Third-Party Alliance Web

Pakistan's Alliances That Encircle India

Every major treaty Pakistan signs with a third country is a geopolitical variable for India. China, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and the US have all signed agreements that directly reshape the India-Pakistan power equation.

MOST CRITICAL
🇨🇳
China-Pakistan: "All-Weather Strategic Partnership"

China-Pakistan FTA (2006, upgraded 2020), CPEC ($62B), defence MoUs, JF-17 fighter jet co-production, joint naval exercises, and 2024 joint statement pledging deeper coordination on Kashmir. 81% of Pakistan's 2024 arms imports came from China.

NEW 2025
🇸🇦
Saudi-Pakistan SMDA: The "Islamic NATO"

Strategic Mutual Defence Agreement (Sept 2025) — any aggression against one = aggression against both. Pakistan provides nuclear deterrence; Saudi provides $billions. India is actively monitoring. Could extend nuclear umbrella to Saudi Arabia.

GROWING
🇹🇷
Turkey-Pakistan Defence Industrial MoU

Joint KAAN 5th-gen fighter discussions, Bayraktar drone deal (2023), trilateral defence-industrial forum with Saudi Arabia. Turkey sided with Pakistan during the May 2025 India-Pakistan confrontation, blocking Indian equipment transit.

HISTORIC
🇺🇸
Pakistan-US: TIFA + Military Aid (Now Strained)

Trade and Investment Framework Agreement (2003). US gave Pakistan $33B+ in aid 2001–2018. Now deeply strained. US-India strategic partnership (defence deals, nuclear agreement) effectively ended Pakistan's "most favored ally" status with Washington.

ACTIVE
🇦🇿
Pakistan-Azerbaijan-Turkey: Arms Triangle

Pakistan signed BIT with Azerbaijan + preferential trade. Azerbaijan supplied arms to Pakistan; Turkey transferred Bayraktar drones. This triangle forms an anti-India corridor that supported Armenia's adversary during Nagorno-Karabakh — while India supported Armenia.

INDIA COUNTER
🇦🇪
UAE Breaks Rank — Signals India Alignment

The UAE — historically close to Pakistan — pivoted toward India. UAE President visited India days after the Saudi-Pakistan SMDA, widely seen as a counter-signal. India-UAE CEPA (2022) gave India a critical Gulf ally inside the Arab world.

Section 05 — Strategic Consequences

How Pakistan's Treaties Impact India

Each treaty Pakistan signs is a strategic variable in India's security calculus. The cumulative effect of China-backed CPEC, a new Saudi defence pact, Turkey's drones, and the collapse of all bilateral agreements creates a uniquely dangerous threat landscape for India in 2025.

EXISTENTIAL
💧
Indus Waters Collapse — Water as Weapon

India suspended the 1960 IWT in April 2025 after Pahalgam attack. Pakistan calls this "weaponization of water" and an "act of war." The Indus basin irrigates ~80% of Pakistan's agricultural land. India's Home Minister vowed it will "never" be restored. Both nations are now in uncharted legal territory — no provision in IWT allows unilateral suspension.

CRITICAL THREAT
☢️
Nuclear Umbrella Expansion via SMDA

The Sept 2025 Saudi-Pakistan SMDA creates implicit nuclear backing for Saudi Arabia. Analysts warn this alters India's security calculus — India now faces a potential nuclear-armed adversary (Pakistan) with a formal mutual defence treaty with a major Gulf power. India is "carefully monitoring" the development.

CRITICAL THREAT
🛣️
CPEC Through Disputed Territory

CPEC runs through Gilgit-Baltistan — territory India claims. China's $62B investment creates permanent infrastructure in India's claimed territory, gives China an Arabian Sea port (Gwadar) bypassing India, and embeds Chinese military contractors and intelligence operatives inside Pakistan.

HIGH THREAT
✈️
Turkish Drones + JF-17 Jets vs Indian Airpower

Pakistan's 2023 Bayraktar TB2 drone deal with Turkey and China-Pakistan JF-17 co-production have shifted the air power equation. Turkey blocked Indian equipment transit during May 2025 confrontation. Pakistan now operates a coalition-supplied multi-source air force designed specifically to challenge Indian aerial superiority.

HIGH THREAT
⚖️
Collapse of All Bilateral Treaty Framework

As of May 2025: IWT suspended, Simla Agreement in abeyance, Wagah border closed, trade halted, diplomatic ties downgraded, Kartarpur Corridor shut. For the first time since 1947, there is no operational bilateral framework between India and Pakistan — making miscalculation by two nuclear states far more likely.

HIGH THREAT
🕌
OIC Mobilisation Against India on Kashmir

Pakistan uses its position in the Organisation of Islamic Cooperation (57 member states) to table anti-India resolutions on Kashmir annually. China-Pakistan joint statements explicitly support Pakistan on Kashmir at the UN Security Council. This multilateral treaty network gives Pakistan diplomatic leverage far beyond its bilateral capacity.

MEDIUM THREAT
🌊
Gwadar Port — Arabian Sea Chokepoint

CPEC's terminal is Gwadar — a deep-sea port just 70km from Iran, commanding the Arabian Sea and Gulf of Oman. China's presence here through treaty arrangements with Pakistan threatens India's naval access to the Persian Gulf and creates a potential dual-use military base at India's western flank.

MEDIUM THREAT
🏔️
China-Pakistan Military Exercises Near LAC

Annual "Warrior" series of China-Pakistan joint military exercises increasingly simulate high-altitude mountain warfare — directly relevant to both the LAC (China-India border) and LoC (India-Pakistan). Pakistan's military treaties with China embed two-front war planning against India.

INDIA COUNTER
🇮🇳
India's Responses: Isolation + Leverage

India suspended IWT and Simla Agreement, closed Wagah, halted trade, downgraded diplomacy, launched "Operation Sindoor" (May 2025 strikes), and engaged Quad + US against Pakistan. UAE's pivot toward India and Israel's drone supply to India create a counter-coalition to the Pakistan-China-Turkey axis.

Analytical Insight — Two-Front War Doctrine

"Pakistan's treaty network — CPEC with China, SMDA with Saudi Arabia, drone deals with Turkey, and OIC multilateralism — is not incidental diplomacy. It is a deliberate effort to ensure that any conflict with India activates a web of political, economic, and military commitments from powerful third parties. For India, the threat is no longer bilateral; it is a multi-vector coalition challenge."

Section 06 — Doctrinal Analysis

Pakistan vs India: Treaty Doctrines Compared

The philosophical divide between how Pakistan and India use international law is as deep as the political divide between them. Pakistan uses treaties as crisis tools; India uses them as institutional anchors.

Core Treaty Philosophy Security-first: treaties as military alliances and crisis management tools Normative multilateralism: treaties as institutional frameworks
Approach to Bilateral Agreements Selective compliance; places agreements "in abeyance" when convenient Upholds until breaking point (IWT survived 4 wars before 2025 suspension)
FTAs in Force ~6 (China, Malaysia, Sri Lanka, Indonesia, Turkey, GCC block) ~13 (ASEAN, Japan, Korea, UAE, Australia, etc.)
Primary Military Alliance China (JF-17, CPEC, joint exercises) + Saudi Arabia SMDA 2025 No formal alliances; strategic partnerships with US, France, Israel, Japan
Nuclear Doctrine in Treaties Uses nuclear status as implicit leverage; extending deterrence to Saudi Arabia via SMDA No First Use policy; nuclear outside most treaty frameworks (not NPT signatory)
Kashmir in Treaty Framework Actively internationalises Kashmir via OIC, UN, China joint statements Insists Kashmir is bilateral (Simla Agreement) — now abandoned by Pakistan
Water Treaties IWT seen as existential — calls any breach "act of war" Suspended IWT 2025; argues treaty unfairly favours Pakistan; wants renegotiation
Arms Import Strategy 81% from China (2024); diversifying to Turkey, Azerbaijan Diversified: Russia, France, US, Israel, domestically produced
Dispute Settlement Prefers multilateral forums (UN, ICJ) where OIC votes give it numerical majority Prefers bilateral resolution; resistant to third-party arbitration on Kashmir
Treaty Reliability Lahore Declaration (1999) broken within months by Kargil; Simla now suspended More consistent; 2025 IWT suspension is unprecedented, first such move
PIL Doctrine — Pacta Sunt Servanda vs Force Majeure

"Pakistan's placement of the Simla Agreement 'in abeyance' and India's suspension of the IWT both invoke the principle of changed circumstances — rebus sic stantibus — to escape treaty obligations. Neither can legally terminate unilaterally. This is the classic tension in Public International Law between the binding nature of pacta sunt servanda and the sovereign's claim to security-based derogation."

Final Analytical Conclusion

"Pakistan's treaty architecture is a masterclass in using international law as a shield and a sword simultaneously — invoking treaties when they protect sovereignty (Simla Agreement on Kashmir internationalisation), abandoning them when they impose costs (Lahore Declaration after Kargil, bilateral framework after Pahalgam), and weaponizing new ones (SMDA with Saudi Arabia) to extend deterrence. For India, understanding this pattern is not merely academic — it is the foundation of any coherent response strategy."
