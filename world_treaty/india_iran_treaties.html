Section 01 — Historical Record

India–Iran Treaty Timeline: 1950–2025

India–Iran ties are shaped by three forces in constant tension: civilizational depth (shared Persian heritage, Shia Islam in India), strategic complementarity (Chabahar, INSTC, Afghan stability), and Western sanction regimes that periodically freeze what treaty frameworks enable.

Section 02 — Bilateral Framework

The Sanctions Paradox

"India and Iran have signed more treaties than they have implemented. The architecture is built; the wiring is cut. US secondary sanctions function as an extra-territorial veto on India's treaty obligations with Iran — forcing India to choose between its sovereign bilateral commitments and its strategic relationship with Washington. This is the defining tension of the India–Iran framework in PIL terms."

Section 03 — Chabahar: The Strategic Centrepiece

Chabahar Port: India's Gateway to Eurasia

Chabahar is not just a port deal — it is India's most important connectivity treaty of the last decade. It gives India a land route to Afghanistan, Central Asia, and Russia that bypasses Pakistan entirely, and anchors the INSTC corridor.

Strategic
⚓
Chabahar Port — Phase I (2016)
PM Modi visits Iran in May 2016. India agrees to develop Shahid Beheshti Port's first phase. Six mobile harbour cranes were provided by India in 2021. Port operationalised in December 2018.
2024 Upgrade
🔗
10-Year Contract (2024)
India's IPTL signs a 10-year operational contract with Iran in May 2024. Includes $370 million investment. US grants a specific sanctions exemption for Chabahar — recognising its strategic value in countering China's Gwadar.
Corridor
🚂
INSTC — North-South Corridor
Chabahar anchors the INSTC (India-Iran-Russia). Reduces transit time from India to Russia and Central Asia from 40 days to 25 days. Cargo from Russia, Brazil, Germany, Bangladesh, Thailand, and UAE has already transited.
Constraint
⚠️
US Sanctions Pressure
Despite the sanctions exemption, many Indian banks refuse Chabahar-related transactions fearing US secondary sanctions. Payment mechanisms remain a critical bottleneck preventing full operationalisation of the treaty.
Geopolitical
🗺️
Gwadar vs Chabahar
China's Gwadar port in Pakistan is 72km from Chabahar. India's Chabahar investment is explicitly designed to provide an alternative to China's influence over the Arabian Sea gateway to Central Asia.
Trilateral
🤝
India–Iran–Uzbekistan Dialogue
Two rounds of trilateral meetings held between India, Iran and Uzbekistan to expand INSTC beyond Afghanistan. Creates a new transit framework that would make the corridor viable even with post-Taliban Afghan instability.
Section 04 — Sanctions: The Treaty Killer

How US Sanctions Reshape India–Iran Treaties

India was Iran's second-largest oil customer (16.5% of crude imports in 2008–09) before sanctions forced a halt in 2019. This compression has redefined almost every economic treaty between India and Iran, replacing formal banking mechanisms with informal rupee-rial systems and barter.

PIL Doctrine — Sovereignty vs Extra-territorial Jurisdiction

"US sanctions on Iran represent one of the most contested questions in modern Public International Law: can one state's domestic legislation lawfully constrain the treaty-based obligations of third parties? India has consistently rejected the principle of extra-territorial jurisdiction while pragmatically complying — a classic case of states choosing economic survival over legal principle."

Final PIL Conclusion

"India–Iran treaties demonstrate that state consent and formal signature are necessary but not sufficient conditions for treaty performance under contemporary international law. The Iran case shows how non-state legal instruments (US executive orders, OFAC designations) can effectively override bilateral treaties between sovereign states — a phenomenon with no clear remedy in the UN Charter or Vienna Convention on the Law of Treaties. India's legal position — objecting in principle, complying in practice — is a study in sovereign pragmatism."
