Impact on India

How Bangladesh's Treaties Affect India

Bangladesh's diplomatic choices — especially its China engagement, water agreements, connectivity frameworks, and trade policies — have direct, measurable consequences for India's security, economy, and regional influence.

🌊
Water / River Treaties
Ganges Water Treaty (1996) ensures India's minimum river-flow commitment to Bangladesh. Teesta remains unsigned — a friction point affecting India's relations with Bangladesh and West Bengal's domestic politics.
Ganges Treaty 1996
🔌
Energy Exports to BD
India exports 1,160 MW of electricity to Bangladesh — making Bangladesh India's largest power export market. Three interconnection agreements and the Adani Godda plant cement energy interdependence.
1,160 MW Exported
🚢
Transit / Connectivity
Bangladesh granted India transit rights through its territory to reach Northeast India — cutting road distance by 1,400 km. The 2015 Protocol on Inland Water Transit and Bangladesh–Bhutan–India–Nepal (BBIN) MVA are transformative.
−1,400 km Distance
💰
Trade Surplus for India
India runs a massive trade surplus with Bangladesh — exporting ~$11.6B against imports of ~$1.3B in 2023. Bangladesh is India's largest trade partner in South Asia and a critical export market for Indian textiles, machinery, and pharma.
$10B+ Surplus
🔐
Security Cooperation
Anti-terrorism Cooperation Agreement (2019), joint border management, and ULFA militant handovers by Bangladesh have materially improved India's Northeast security environment. Cooperation on trafficking and narcotics is ongoing.
6 Security Pacts
🇨🇳
China Factor (Risk)
Bangladesh's BRI participation (2016), Chinese port investments at Chittagong and Payra, and Dhaka's deepening military ties with Beijing create strategic anxiety for India — a classic "string of pearls" encirclement concern.
$38B BRI Pledge
