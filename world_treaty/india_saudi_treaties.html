Section 01 — Historical Record

India–Saudi Arabia Treaty Timeline: 1947–2025

India–Saudi Arabia's treaty relationship transformed dramatically after 2006. From a purely energy-transactional relationship, it has evolved into a comprehensive strategic partnership with the SPC (Strategic Partnership Council) as its institutional anchor — and a $100B investment commitment as its most audacious expression.

Section 03 — The SPC: India's Most Ambitious Gulf Framework

Strategic Partnership Council: Anatomy of a Mega-Relationship

The 2019 SPC Agreement is the most institutionally sophisticated bilateral framework India has established with any Gulf country. It creates a two-tier council with sub-committees, ministerial meetings, senior officials meetings, and joint working groups — modelled on India's most advanced partnerships globally.

Architecture
🏛️
SPC Structure: 4 Ministerial Committees
After April 2025 expansion: (1) Political, Consular & Security Cooperation; (2) Defence Cooperation; (3) Economy, Energy, Investment & Technology; (4) Culture, Education, Healthcare & Diaspora. Each has JWGs and senior official mechanisms.
Energy
🛢️
$50B Energy Investment Package
Saudi Arabia committed $50B toward energy projects in India (Apr 2025). Includes 2 oil refineries (9 MMT capacity each) with Saudi Aramco taking 26% equity stake. Saudi Arabia: India's 3rd largest crude supplier (14.3% of imports).
Defence
⚔️
$15B Defence Collaboration Package
Defence Cooperation Committee formed under SPC (2025). Joint military exercises (Al Mohed Al Hindi naval exercise). $225M Munitions India Ltd export deal (2024). Technology transfer and drone surveillance cooperation discussed.
Investment
💰
$100B Investment Commitment
First announced during MBS's 2019 visit; reaffirmed in 2025. KIA, PIF (Public Investment Fund), and private Saudi capital targeting Indian infrastructure, tech startups, AI, biotech, and renewable energy sectors.
Diaspora
👥
2.2 Million Indians — Second Largest Gulf Diaspora
2.2M Indians in Saudi Arabia — second largest Gulf diaspora after UAE. Saudi Arabia has eased Police Clearance Certificate requirement for Indians (2022). Largest source of remittances in Saudi Arabia from any single country.
Hajj
🕌
Annual Hajj Agreement — Religious Diplomacy
India has the largest Hajj quota of any country: 175,025 pilgrims in 2024. Annual bilateral Hajj Agreement is a unique treaty instrument — no other bilateral relationship blends religious access with formal governmental agreements in this way.
Strategic Weight

"The India–Saudi Strategic Partnership Council represents a deliberate institutional elevation — transforming what was essentially an energy-and-diaspora relationship into a comprehensive strategic framework. With $41.9B in annual bilateral trade, a $100B investment pledge, and a new defence committee, India and Saudi Arabia have built the Gulf's most consequential bilateral relationship — one that now shapes both countries' global positioning."

Section 05 — PIL Analysis

What Saudi Arabia's Treaty Pattern Reveals

India–Saudi Arabia demonstrates a treaty strategy where soft-law instruments (MoUs, declarations) have outpaced hard-law agreements (binding treaties). The SPC is not a treaty — it is a political declaration creating institutional mechanisms. This reflects both countries' preference for flexibility over legally binding obligations.

PIL Conclusion — Institutionalised Soft Law

"The India–Saudi relationship is a case study in institutionalised soft law — a vast network of MoUs, declarations, and councils that create political commitment without legal obligation. Unlike a treaty (pacta sunt servanda), MoUs are non-binding in international law. Yet the SPC's institutional architecture creates what PIL scholars call 'compliance pull' — the political cost of non-performance becomes equivalent to treaty breach. India has deliberately chosen this model with Saudi Arabia to preserve strategic flexibility while deepening commitment."
